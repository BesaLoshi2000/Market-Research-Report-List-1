<p><p>The organic rheology modifiers market is categorized into two main types: cellulosics and synthetics. Cellulosics are derived from natural cellulose and are known for their thickening, stabilizing, and emulsifying properties, making them widely used in coatings, cosmetics, and food industries. Synthetics, on the other hand, are chemically engineered materials that provide enhanced performance characteristics such as heat resistance and superior flow control. Both types play a crucial role in optimizing viscosity and enhancing the functionality of various formulations.</p></p>
