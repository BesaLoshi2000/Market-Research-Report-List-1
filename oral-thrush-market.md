<p><ul><li>Pseudomembranous</li><li>Erythematous</li><li>Hyperplastic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Oral thrush presents in three main market types based on its clinical manifestations. Pseudomembranous thrush features white, creamy lesions that can be wiped away, often indicating severe infection. Erythematous thrush appears as red, inflamed areas, typically lacking white patches, signaling a more painful form. Hyperplastic thrush is characterized by thick, white lesions that cannot be easily removed, suggesting a more chronic condition. Each type reflects differing severity and symptoms, influencing treatment approaches and market focus for healthcare solutions.</p></p>
