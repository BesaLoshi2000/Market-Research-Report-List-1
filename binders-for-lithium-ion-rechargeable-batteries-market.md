<p><p>The binders for lithium-ion rechargeable batteries play a crucial role in the performance and stability of battery electrodes. Anode binders, typically made from materials like sodium carboxymethyl cellulose or polyvinylidene fluoride, enhance the adhesion of active materials in the anode, supporting better charge transfer and cycling stability. Cathode binders, often polyvinylidene fluoride or other polymers, serve a similar purpose for the cathode, ensuring structural integrity and efficient electron conduction. Both types are essential for improving overall battery efficiency and longevity.</p></p>
