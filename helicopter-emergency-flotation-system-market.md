<p><p>The Helicopter Emergency Flotation System (HEFS) market is segmented into two main types: life-limited and no life-limit systems. Life-limited HEFS are designed for a specific operational lifespan and require periodic inspections or replacements. These systems are often more cost-effective but may necessitate frequent maintenance. In contrast, no life-limit systems are engineered for extended durability, offering enhanced reliability and reduced long-term costs, as they do not require replacement within a specified timeframe, thereby appealing to operators seeking longevity and lower maintenance.</p></p>
