<p><p>Cross Laminated Timber (CLT) is an engineered wood product made by stacking layers of lumber at alternating angles, providing strength and stability for various construction applications. The market for CLT is divided into adhesive-bonded and mechanically fastened types. Adhesive-bonded CLT utilizes strong adhesives to bond layers, ensuring uniform integrity and moisture resistance. In contrast, mechanically fastened CLT employs screws and nails for assembly, offering quicker installation and reusability. Both types cater to sustainable building practices and increasing demand in the construction industry.</p></p>
