<p><ul><li>Networked Systems</li><li>Standalone Systems</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Keypad electronic access control systems are categorized into two main types: networked systems and standalone systems. Networked systems connect multiple access points to a centralized management system, allowing real-time monitoring, user management, and integration with other security features. In contrast, standalone systems operate independently, managing access at a single location without external connectivity. Both types serve to enhance security through keypad entry, but networked systems offer greater scalability and control for larger facilities, while standalone systems provide simplicity for smaller applications.</p></p>
